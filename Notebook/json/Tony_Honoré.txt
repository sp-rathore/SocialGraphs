He served in the army during the [[Second World War]] and was severely wounded in the [[First Battle of El Alamein|Battle of Alamein]]. After the war he continued his studies at [[New College, Oxford]], and he has lived and taught in [[Oxford, England|Oxford]] for the last fifty-five years, including periods as a [[Oxbridge Fellow|Fellow]] of [[The Queen's College, Oxford|The Queen's College]] and then of New College.<ref name=\"AllSouls\">Profile of [http://www.all-souls.ox.ac.uk/people.php?personid=150 Tony Honré] All Souls College website, Oxford; read April 1st, 2014.</ref> Between 1971 and 1988 he was [[Regius Professor of Civil Law (Oxford)|Regius Professor of Civil Law at Oxford]] and a Fellow of [[All Souls College, Oxford]];<ref name=\"OxfordProfile\">Profile of [https://www.law.ox.ac.uk/profile/hornorea Tony Honoré] University of Oxford website, read April 1st, 2014.</ref><ref name=\"SKU 978-1-920025-38-0\">Daniel Visser and Max Loubser (2011) ''[http://www.siberink.co.za/p978-1-920025-38-0/Thinking-about-Law-Essays-for-Tony-Honore.aspx Thinking about Law: Essays for Tony Honore]''; Siber Ink, Westlake (South Africa). read April 1st, 2014.</ref> though retired from his Chair, he teaches seminars in Jurisprudence for the [[Bachelor of Civil Law|BCL]] jointly with [[John Gardner (law)|John Gardner]].\n\nHonoré was a close associate of [[H. L. A. Hart]]. They jointly wrote ''Causation in the Law'' (Oxford, 1st ed. 1959, 2nd 1985) in 1953-8 and Honoré had some influence on Hart's ''The Concept of Law'' (Oxford, 1st ed. 1961, 2nd 1994). A number of his philosophical papers are collected in ''Making Law Bind'' (Oxford, 1987) and ''Responsibility and Fault'' (Oxford, 1999) and his contributions to legal philosophy and Roman law, which range widely, include sixteen books and more than a hundred articles published over the last six decades.\n\nHonoré is an honorary [[Queen's Counsel|Q.C.]] and Bencher of [[Lincoln's Inn]], a member of the [[British Academy|British]] and [[Bavarian Academy of Sciences and Humanities|Bavarian Academies]] and of the International Academy of Comparative Law. In South Africa, where he made a contribution to the setting up of the new [[Constitutional Court of South Africa|Constitutional Court]] in 1995, his standing has been recognised by the award of honorary degrees from the Universities of [[University of South Africa|South Africa]], [[Stellenbosch University|Stellenbosch]] and [[University of Cape Town|Cape Town]]. He has delivered the Hamlyn lectures (1982),<ref>''The Quest for security: Employees, Tenants, Wives'', Tony Honoré [http://socialsciences.exeter.ac.uk/media/universityofexeter/schoolofhumanitiesandsocialsciences/law/pdfs/The_Quest_for_Security_Employees_Tenants_Wives_1.pdf]</ref> the Blackstone and H. L. A. Hart lectures, the J. H. Gray lectures at [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]] and the Maccabaean lecture in Jurisprudence at the British Academy.
