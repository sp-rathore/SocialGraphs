Gasset]]\n|influenced       = [[Ignacio Ellacuría]]\n|signature        = \n}}\n\n'''Xavier Zubiri''' ([[Donostia-San Sebastián]], [[Gipuzkoa]], [[Spain]], 4 December 1898 – [[Madrid]], 21 September 1983) was a [[Spain|Spanish]] [[philosopher]].\n\n== Biography ==\nHe was a member of the [[School of Madrid]], composed by philosophers [[José Ortega y Gasset]], [[Julián Marías]] and [[Pedro Laín Entralgo]], among others.<ref>{{Citation|title=La evolución de la escuela de Madrid (1939-1978): Zubiri, Marías y Laín Entralgo|curly=|url=http://pendientedemigracion.ucm.es/centros/cont/descargas/documento34341.pdf|work=José Luis Cañas Fernández|year=2012|accessdate=2013-10-15|language=es}}</ref> Zubiri's philosophy has been categorized as a \"materialist open realism\",<ref>{{Citation|title=Voluntad de Liberacion|curly=|url=http://www.uca.edu.sv/filosofia/admin/files/1203712190.pdf|work=Hector Samour|year=2003|accessdate=2013-10-15|language=es}}</ref> which \"attempted to reformulate classical metaphysics, in a language that was entirely compatible with modern science\".<ref>{{Citation|title=La filosofía natural de Zubiri|curly=|url=http://digital.csic.es/bitstream/10261/10144/1/La%20filosof%C3%ADa%20natural%20de%20Zubiri.pdf|work=José Luis González Quirós|year=1997|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref> This relates to Xavier Zubiri's educational background. Zubiri first received a philosophical and theological formation in [[Madrid]] and [[Rome]]. Later, he deepend his studies in philosophy through his graduate studies in [[Leuven|Louvain]], writing his dissertation on [[phenomenology (philosophy)|phenomenology]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Le problème de l'objectivité d'après Ed. Husserl|curly=|url=https://services.libis.be/query?query=sys:LBS01000334602&view=KULeuven&institution=KUL&builder=primo_opensearch&host=limo.libis.be|work=Xavier Zubiri|date=1921–1922|accessdate=2013-10-15|language=fr}}</ref> In 1929, Zubiri's critical interest in this current of thought took him to [[Freiburg]], when he already was a professor in Madrid. There, he studied with [[Edmund Husserl]] and [[Martin Heidegger]]. In 1930, Zubiri moved to [[Berlin]], where he studied [[physics]], [[philology]] and [[biology]]. There, he was hosted in [[Harnack House]], which enabled Zubiri to socialize with important minds of this great period of academic activity in the [[Weimar republic]]. For example, [[Albert Einstein]] (whom Zubiri had already met in Madrid, at Universidad Central, in 1923),<ref>{{Citation|title=Conología del viaje de Einstein a España|curly=|url=http://quark.prbb.org/36/036054.pdf|work=Quark, Ciencia, Medicina, Comunicación y Cultura. Publicación del Observatorio de Comunicación Científica de la Universitat Pomeu Fabra|date=May–August 2005|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref> [[Max Planck]], [[Werner Jaeger]], [[Erwin Schrödinger]], among others. \n\nWhen [[Spanish civil war|civil war]] broke out in Spain in 1936, Zubiri moved to Paris. There, he continued having an intensive intellectual life, attending courses with [[Louis de Broglie]], [[Frédéric Joliot]], [[Irène Curie]], [[Elie Joseph Cartan]] and [[Emile Benveniste]], among others. In 1939, just before France declared war to Germany, Zubiri returned to Spain.<ref>{{Citation|title=Xavier Zubiri: La soledad sonora|curly=|url=http://www.editorialtaurus.com/uploads/ficheros/libro/primeras-paginas/200603/primeras-paginas-xavier-zubiri.pdf|work=Jordi Corominas and Joan Vicens|year=2006|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Biografía de Xavier Zubiri|curly=|url=https://sites.google.com/site/corominasescude/Home/escrits-i-publicacions/presentacion/biografia-de-xavier-zubiri|work=Jordi Corominas and Joan Vicens|year=2006|accessdate=2013-10-19|language=es}}</ref> Zubiri's philosophy is little known outside of [[Spain]] and [[Latin America]], mostly because Zubiri was compelled to resign from formal academic positions in [[Spain]], in 1942. This had to do with the lack of academic freedoms in [[Francisco Franco]]'s regime.<ref>{{Citation|title=Zubiri, vasco universal|curly=|url=http://elpais.com/diario/1980/10/10/opinion/339980408_850215.html|work=Ignacio Ellacuría|date=1980-10-10|accessdate=2013-10-19|language=es}}</ref> However, it was possible for Zubiri to continue his work as an [[academic]], through the sponsorship of family and friends.<ref>{{Citation|title=Xavier Zubiri: La soledad sonora|curly=|url=http://books.google.no/books?id=1aUQAQAAIAAJ&q=Xavier+Zubiri:+La+Soledad+Sonora&dq=Xavier+Zubiri:+La+Soledad+Sonora&hl=no&sa=X&ei=av1gUpa3OJGQ4ASu7oDQAw&ved=0CC8Q6AEwAA|work=Jordi Corominas and Joan Vicens|year=2006|accessdate=2013-10-15|language=es}}</ref>  Zubiri was a prolific author in the Spanish magazines ''[[Cruz y Raya]]'' (led by [[José Bergamín]])  and ''[[Revista de Occidente]]'' (led by [[José Ortega y Gasset]]) under the [[second Spanish republic]]. However, after his resignation from Spanish universities, Zubiri did not publish much in established [[peer reviewed]] journals. Nonetheless, he did publish a series of books and research articles.<ref>{{Citation|title=Xavier Zubiri: La soledad sonora, Chapter 14, 15 and 39 on \"La República\" (The Republic) (pp. 245-265), \"Cruz y Raya\" (the magazine) (pp. 265 - 287) and \"El duro oficio de escribir\" (The hard task of writing) (pp. 589-606), respectively|curly=|url=http://books.google.no/books/about/Xavier_Zubiri.html?id=1aUQAQAAIAAJ&redir_esc=y|work=Jordi Corominas and Joan Vicens|year=2006|accessdate=2014-12-12|language=es}}</ref> Zubiri's work was initially not well received by established academic environments in Spain. This was mostly explained by the political context under Franco.<ref>{{Citation|title=Zubiri tras el problema de toma de posición tras el comienzo de la guerra civil |curly= |url=http://www.javier.org.es/archivos/enlaces/_private/trabajos/exiliozubiri1.htm |work=Javier Borrego Gutierrez |year=2003 |accessdate=2013-10-18 |language=es |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131022164339/http://www.javier.org.es/archivos/enlaces/_private/trabajos/exiliozubiri1.htm |archivedate=October 22, 2013 }}</ref> But Zubiri's relationship to scholars like [[Ignacio Ellacuria]] made Zubiri's work widely known in Latin America, where Zubiri's thought has been further developed.<ref>{{Citation|title=Actualidad de Zubiri en América Latina|curly=|url=http://dialnet.unirioja.es/servlet/articulo?codigo=2293976|work=Germán Marquínez Argote|year=2006|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref> \n\nRecently, Spanish academics have begun to recognize the importance of Zubiri's life and philosophy.<ref>{{Citation|title=Amigos y discípulos de Zubiri reivindican su gigantesca talla intelectual en su centenario|curly=|url=http://elpais.com/diario/1998/12/02/cultura/912553204_850215.html|work=El País|date=1998-12-02|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{Citation|title=Zubiri, hombre, filósofo y fenomenólogo|curly=|url=http://www.uned.es/dpto_fim/InvFen/InvFen05/pdf/07_SAN_MARTIN.pdf|work=Javier San Martin|year=2006|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref>\nFor the same reasons outlined above, Zubiri's contact with the formal academic environments of the English speaking world was limited. There is all but one recorded visit by Zubiri to the [[United States]], specifically [[Princeton University]], on October 2, 1946. In Princeton, Zubiri lectured in French on \"The real and mathematics- A philosophical problem\" (\"Le reel et les mathematiques—Un probleme de philosophie\").<ref>{{Citation|title=Daily Princetonian, Volume 70, Number 105, 2 October 1946|curly=|url=http://theprince.princeton.edu/princetonperiodicals/cgi-bin/princetonperiodicals?a=d&d=Princetonian19461002-01.2.18&srpos=1&e=-------en-20--1--txt-IN-zubiri----#|work=Official Notices|date=1946-10-02|accessdate=2013-10-18}}</ref>  Some of Zubiri's work has been translated to English: \"On Essence\" (Caponigri, 1980),<ref>{{Citation|title=On Essence|curly=|url=http://books.google.no/books/about/On_Essence.html?id=VZ3WAAAAMAAJ&redir_esc=y|work=Xavier Zubiri, translated by A. Robert Caponigri|year=1980|accessdate=2013-10-18}}</ref> \"Sentient Intelligence\" (Fowler, 1999),<ref>{{Citation|title=Sentient Intelligence|curly=|url=http://www.zubiri.org/works/englishworks/si/Sentientintelligence.pdf|work=Xavier Zubiri, translated by Thomas B. Fowler, Sc.D. |year=1999|accessdate=2013-10-18}}</ref> \"The Dynamic Structure of Reality\" (Orringer, 2003)<ref>{{Citation|title=The Dynamic Structure of Reality|curly=|url=http://books.google.no/books/about/Dynamic_Structure_of_Reality.html?id=BMlYYu52w0IC&redir_esc=y|work=Xavier Zubiri, translated by Nelson Orringer|year=2003|accessdate=2013-10-18}}</ref> and \"The Fundamental problems of Western Metaphysics\" (Redondo & Fowler 2009).<ref>{{Citation|title=The Fundamental Problems of Western Metaphysics|curly=|url=http://books.google.no/books?id=n1GAFDXd5boC&source=gbs_similarbooks|work=Xavier Zubiri, translated by Joaquín Redondo and Thomas Fowler|year=2009|accessdate=2013-10-19}}</ref> Despite his relative academic isolation at home in Spain, Zubiri has also been recognized in other countries. In 1979, the German government awarded Zubiri and Laín Entralgo the [[Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany]]. Zubiri was awarded this distinction for his work in his books \"Nature, History, God\" (1954) and \"On Essence\" (1962).<ref>{{Citation|title=El Gobierno alemán condecora a Zubiri y a Laín|curly=|url=http://elpais.com/diario/1979/12/04/cultura/313110004_850215.html|date=1979-12-04|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref> Zubiri's work has also been translated to [[French language|French]], [[German language|German]] and [[Italian language|Italian]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Traducciones de las obras de Zubiri a otros idiomas|curly=|url=http://www.zubiri.net/idiomas.html|work=Funación Xavier Zubiri|accessdate=2013-10-18|language=es}}</ref>\n\n== Published Books ==\n\nThis overview is taken from a more extensive list of articles and books by and about Xavier Zubiri.
