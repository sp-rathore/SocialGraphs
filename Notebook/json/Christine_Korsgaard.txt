She has taught at [[Yale University|Yale]], the [[University of California at Santa Barbara]], and the [[University of Chicago]]; since 1991 she has been a professor at [[Harvard University]], where she is now Arthur Kingsley Porter Professor of Philosophy.  She has been described as \"one of today's leading moral philosophers\"<ref>https://ndpr.nd.edu/news/24047-the-constitution-of-agency-essays-on-practical-reason-and-moral-psychology/</ref> because of her work in defense of [[Kantian ethics|Kantian views in moral theory]].\n\n==Biography==\nKorsgaard first attended Eastern Illinois University for two years and transferred to receive a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] from the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign|University of Illinois]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D]] from Harvard, where she was a student of [[John Rawls]].
