Soon after, Summers held a private meeting with West, where he reportedly rebuked West for missing too many classes, contributing to [[grade inflation]], neglecting serious scholarship, and spending too much time on his economically profitable projects.<ref>{{cite news |agency=Associated Press |title=Who is Cornel West? |url=http://archives.cnn.com/2002/fyi/teachers.ednews/01/10/west.harvard.ap/ |publisher=[[CNN|Cable News Network]] |date= January 10, 2002 |accessdate=January 21, 2008 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071226113730/http://archives.cnn.com/2002/fyi/teachers.ednews/01/10/west.harvard.ap/ <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate=December 26, 2007}}</ref> Summers reportedly suggested that West produce an academic book befitting his professorial position, as his recent output had consisted primarily of co-written and edited volumes. According to some reports, Summers also objected to West's production of a CD, the critically panned ''Sketches of My Culture'', and to his political campaigning, including an alleged three weeks to promote [[Bill Bradley]]'s [[Democratic Party (United States) presidential primaries, 2000|presidential]] campaign.<ref>{{cite news |first=Jacques |last=Steinberg |authorlink=Jacques Steinberg |title=At Odds With Harvard President, Black-Studies Stars Eye Princeton |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9F06E4D71F31F93AA15751C1A9679C8B63 |work=The New York Times |publisher=The New York Times Company |location=New York City |date=November 29, 2001 |accessdate=January 21, 2008}}</ref> West contended he had missed only one class during his tenure at Harvard \"in order to give a keynote address at a Harvard-sponsored conference on AIDS.\" Summers also allegedly suggested that since West held the rank of [[Harvard University Professor]] and thus reported directly to the President, he should meet with Summers regularly to discuss the progress of his academic production.<ref name=\"indignant\">{{cite news |first=Pam |last=Belluck |author2=Jacques Steinberg |title=Defector Indignant at President of Harvard |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9C0DE6DB113CF935A25757C0A9649C8B63 |work=The New York Times |publisher=The New York Times Company |location=New York City |date=April 16, 2002 |accessdate=January 21, 2008}}</ref>\n\nSummers refused to comment on the details of his conversation with West, except to express hope that West would remain at Harvard. United States===\nWest has called the U.S. a \"racist patriarchal\" nation where [[white supremacy]] continues to define everyday life. \"White America,\" he writes, \"has been historically weak-willed in ensuring racial justice and has continued to resist fully accepting the humanity of blacks.\" This has resulted, he says, in the creation of many \"degraded and oppressed people hungry for identity, meaning, and self-worth.\" West attributes most of the black community's problems to \"existential angst derive[d] from the lived experience of ontological wounds and emotional scars inflicted by white supremacist beliefs and images permeating U.S. society and culture.\"<ref>Cornel West, ''Race Matters'', p. 27, 2001 edition, ISBN 978-0-8070-0972-7</ref>\n\nIn West's view, the [[September 11 attacks]] gave white Americans a glimpse of what it means to be a black person in the United States—feeling \"unsafe, unprotected, subject to random violence, and hatred\" for who they |date=September 30, 2015 |work=[[Nation19]] |accessdate=April 23, 2016 }}</ref>\n\n=== Politics ===\nWest has described himself as a \"non-[[Marxist]] socialist\" (partly because he does not view Marxism and Christianity as reconcilable)<ref>{{cite book |last=West |first=Cornel |title=The Cornel West Reader |year=1999 |publisher=Basic ''Civitas'' Books |location=New York |isbn=0-465-09109-1 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bNJD8g1e-QQC&pg=PA13 |page=13 }}</ref> and serves as honorary chair of the [[Democratic Socialists of America]], which he has described as \"the first multiracial, socialist organization close enough to my politics that I could join\".<ref name=blackbio/> He also described himself as a \"radical democrat, suspicious of all forms of authority\" on the ''Matrix''-themed documentary ''The Burly Man Chronicles''.<ref>{{cite video |people=Cornel West |title=[[The Ultimate Matrix Collection]] }}</ref>\n\nWest believes that \"the overthrow of [[Saddam Hussein]]'s ugly totalitarian regime was desirable,\"<ref>Cornel West, ''Democracy Matters,'' p. 58, 2004, ISBN 0-14-303583-5</ref> but that the [[war in Iraq]] was the result of \"dishonest manipulation\" on the part of the Bush administration.<ref>Cornel West, ''Democracy Matters,'' p. 101, 2004, ISBN 0-14-303583-5</ref> He asserts that Bush Administration [[war hawk|hawks]] \"are not simply conservative elites and right-wing ideologues\", but rather are \"evangelical nihilists – drunk with power and driven by grand delusions of American domination of the world\". He adds, \"We are [now] experiencing the sad gangsterization of America, an unbridled grasp at power, wealth, and status.\" Viewing capitalism as the root cause of these alleged American lusts, West warns, \"[[market fundamentalism|Free-market fundamentalism]] trivializes the concern for public interest. It puts fear and insecurity in the hearts of anxiety-ridden workers. It also makes money-driven, poll-obsessed elected officials deferential to corporate goals of profit – often at the cost of the common good.\"<ref>[http://www.theglobalist.com/StoryId.aspx?StoryId=4262 Theglobalist.com]  {{wayback|url=http://www.theglobalist.com/StoryId.aspx?StoryId=4262 |date=20101203073821 }}</ref>\n\nWest has been involved with such projects as the [[Million Man March]] and [[Russell Simmons]]'s Hip-Hop Summit, and worked with such public figures as [[Louis Farrakhan]]<ref name=\"influx\"/> and [[Al Sharpton]], whose 2004 presidential campaign West advised.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/409271497?accountid=35803 |title=Cornel West, Cloaked in Street Smarts |last=Givhan |first=Robin |authorlink=Robin Givhan |date=January 25, 2002 |work=The Washington Post |accessdate=October 22, 2011 }}</ref>\n\nIn 2000, West worked as a senior advisor to [[United States Democratic Party|Democratic]] [[President of the United States|presidential]] candidate [[Bill Bradley]]. When Bradley lost in the primaries, West became a prominent endorser of [[Ralph Nader]], even speaking at some Nader rallies. Some [[United States Green Party|Greens]] sought to draft West to run as a presidential candidate in 2004. West declined, citing his active participation in the [[Al Sharpton]] campaign. West, along with other prominent Nader 2000 supporters, signed the \"Vote to Stop Bush\" statement urging progressive voters in swing states to vote for [[John Kerry]], despite strong disagreements with many of Kerry's policies.<ref>\"Senators Hillary Clinton and John Kerry are exemplary paternalistic nihilists... In 2011, West called on the [[University of Arizona]] to divest from companies profiting from the [[Israeli occupation of the Palestinian territories]].<ref>[http://nomoredeaths.org/University-of-Arizona-NMD/cornel-west-ua-letter.html Nomordeaths.org] {{wayback|url=http://nomoredeaths.org/University-of-Arizona-NMD/cornel-west-ua-letter.html |date=20110719162402 }}</ref>\n\nWest also serves as co-chair of the [[Tikkun Community]]. He co-chaired the National Parenting Organization's Task Force on Parent Empowerment and participated in [[Bill Clinton|President Clinton]]'s National Conversation on Race. He has publicly endorsed ''[[In These Times]]'' magazine by calling it: \"The most creative and challenging news magazine of the American left\". He is also a contributing editor for ''[[Sojourners Magazine]]''.\n\nWest supports [[People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals]] (PETA) in its Kentucky Fried Cruelty campaign, aimed at eliminating what PETA describes as [[KFC]]'s inhumane treatment of chickens. West is quoted on [[PETA]] flyers: \"Although most people don't know chickens as well as they know cats and dogs, chickens are interesting individuals with personalities and interests every bit as developed as the dogs and cats with whom many of us share our lives.\"\n\nIn 2008, West contributed his insights on the current global issue of modernized slavery and [[human trafficking]] in the documentary Call+Response.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.callandresponse.com |title=Call + Response |publisher=Callandresponse.com |accessdate=March 7, 2011}}</ref> West is a member of the [[Campaign for Peace and Democracy]].\n\nIn 2011, West addressed his frustration about some critics of the [[Occupy Wall Street]], who remark about the movement's lack of a clear and unified message. Federal Election Commission]]'' case the previous year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/guest-voices/post/dr-cornel-west-honors-dr-martin-luther-king-jr-by-going-to-jail/2011/10/17/gIQA9oLRrL_blog.html |title=Dr. Cornel West honors Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. by going to jail |author=Thisltethwaite, Susan Brooks |date=October 17, 2011 |work=The Washington Post |publisher= |accessdate=April 21, 2012}}</ref> Five days later, he was arrested during an Occupy Wall Street protest in [[Harlem]] against the New York Police Department's [[stop and frisk]] policy.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://newsfeed.time.com/2011/10/22/cornel-west-arrested-again-at-occupy-protest/ |title=Cornel West Arrested Again at Occupy Protest |author=Carbone, Nick |date=October 22, 2011 |work=Time |publisher= |accessdate=April 21, 2012}}</ref>\n\n===Views on Barack Obama===\nCornel West publicly supported [[2008 Democratic Presidential candidate]] Senator [[Barack Obama]].
