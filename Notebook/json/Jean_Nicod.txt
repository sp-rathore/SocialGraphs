He also proposed the [[Nicod's axiom]] and developed [[Nicod's criterion]].\n\nNicod died at the age of 31 from [[tuberculosis]].\n\nSee also [[Carl Hempel]]'s [[raven paradox]].\n\n== Legacy ==\nThe [[Institut Jean Nicod]] (Paris) &mdash; a branch of the French ''Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique'' ([[CNRS]]) -- is research laboratory at the interface between [[philosophy]], [[cognitive science]], and the [[social science]]s &mdash; was named in honour of Nicod's memory. Jean Nicod's name is also commemorated by the prestigious [[Jean Nicod Prize|Jean Nicod Lectures]], delivered annually in Paris by a leading [[philosopher of mind]] or philosophically oriented [[cognitive scientist]], and published as a series by the [[MIT Press]].
