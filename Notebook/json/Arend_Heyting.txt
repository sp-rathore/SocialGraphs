He was a student of [[Luitzen Egbertus Jan Brouwer]] at the [[University of Amsterdam]], and did much to put [[intuitionistic logic]] on a footing where it could become part of [[mathematical logic]].  Heyting gave the first formal development of intuitionistic logic in order to codify Brouwer's way of doing mathematics.  The inclusion of Brouwer's name in the [[Brouwer–Heyting–Kolmogorov interpretation]] is largely honorific, as Brouwer was opposed in principle to the formalisation of certain intuitionistic principles (and went as far as calling Heyting's work a \"sterile exercise\").<ref>Walter P. van Stigt (1990).
