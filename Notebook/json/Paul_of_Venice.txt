{"batchcomplete": "", "query": {"normalized": [{"to": "Paul of Venice", "from": "Paul_of_Venice"}], "pages": {"2364425": {"ns": 0, "pageid": 2364425, "revisions": [{"*": "[[File:Paolo - Logica, 1546 - 4629053.tif|thumb|''Logica'', 1546]]\n'''Paul of Venice''' (or '''Paulus Venetus''') (1369–1429) was a [[Roman Catholic Church|Roman Catholic]] [[Scholastic philosopher]], [[theologian]], and [[Philosophical realism|realist]] [[logician]] and [[metaphysician]] of the [[Hermits of the Order of Saint Augustine]]. He was teacher to [[Paolo da Pergola]].\n\n==Life==\nHe was born, according to the chroniclers of his order, at [[Udine]], about 1369 and died at [[Venice]] on June 15, 1429, as '''Paolo Nicoletti'''. He joined the [[Augustinians|Augustinian Order]] at around 1383, at the convent of [[Santo Stefano, Venice|Santo Stefano]] in Venice. In 1390 he is said to have been sent to [[Oxford]] for his studies in theology, but returned to [[Italy]], and finished his course at [[Padua]]. He lectured in the [[University at Padua]] during the first quarter of the fifteenth century.\n\nPaul was one of the theologians called to [[Rome]] in 1427 by [[Pope Martin V]], to take cognizance of the charges brought against [[Bernardino of Siena|St. Bernardino of Siena]], occasioned by Bernardino's use of inscriptions of the name of Jesus in worship.\n\n==Realism==\nWith regard to the [[problem of universals]] he adhered to [[Philosophical realism|realism]], since he maintained that universal essences are real entities.\n\n==Works==\nHis writings show a wide knowledge and interest in the [[science|scientific]] problems of his time.\n \n* Commentaries on the works of Aristotle:\n** ''Expositio in libros Posteriorum Aristotelis''.\n** ''Expositio super VIII libros Physicorum necnon super Commento Averrois'' (1409).\n** ''Expositio super libros De generatione et corruptione''.\n** ''Lectura super librum De Anima''.\n** ''Conclusiones Ethicorum''.\n** ''Conclusiones Politicorum''.\n** ''Expositio super Praedicabilia et Praedicamenta'' (1428).\n* Logical works:\n** ''Logica Parva'' or ''Tractatus Summularum'' (1395–96).\n** ''  Logica Magna'' (1397–98).\n** ''Quadratura''.\n** ''Sophismata Aurea''.\n* Other works:\n** ''Super Primum Sententiarum Johannis de Ripa Lecturae Abbreviatio'' (1401).\n** ''Summa philosophiae naturalis'' (1408).\n** ''De compositione mundi''.\n** ''Quaestiones adversus Judaeos''.\n** ''Sermones''.\n\n==English translations==\n* ''Logica Parva.''
