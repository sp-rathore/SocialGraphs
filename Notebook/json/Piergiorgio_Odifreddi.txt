He is philosophically and politically near to [[Bertrand Russell]] and [[Noam Chomsky]].\n\n==Early life and education==\nBorn in [[Cuneo]] in the [[Piedmont]], he received his ''[[Laurea]]'' ''cum laude'' in mathematics in [[Turin]] in 1973. He then specialized in the United States at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign]] and [[UCLA]] from 1978 and 1980, and in the Soviet Union at [[Novosibirsk State University]] in 1982 and 1983.\n\n==Teaching career==\nFrom 1983 to 2007, he taught logic at the [[University of Turin]], and from [[1985]] to [[2003]] he was [[visiting professor]] at [[Cornell University]], where he collaborated with [[Anil Nerode]], Richard Platek, and [[Richard Shore]]. From 2001 to 2003 he taught at ''Università Vita-Salute San Raffaele'', which was founded by Luigi Maria Verzé.<ref>[https://it.wikinews.org/wiki/Intervista_a_Piergiorgio_Odifreddi ''Interview with Piergiorgio Odifreddi'' on Wikinews]</ref>\n\nHe has been visiting professor at [[Monash University]] in [[Melbourne]] in [[1988]], the [[Chinese Academy of Sciences]] in Beijing in 1992 and in [[1995]], the ''Università di Nanchino'' in [[1998]], Buenos Aires Universitt in 2001 and the ''Italian Academy'' at [[Columbia University]] in [[2006]].<ref>[http://odifreddi.blogautore.repubblica.it/2011/12/21/da-montesquieu-a-napolitano/comment-page-3/#comment-35898 Il Non-Senso della vita - Blog di Piergiorgio Odifreddi]</ref>\n\n==Writing==\nHe has written editorials and books reviews for ''La rivista dei libri'' (the Italian edition of the [[New York Review of Books]]), is a regular contributor to ''[[Le Scienze]]'' (the Italian edition of [[Scientific American]]), and has also written for several newspapers such as [[La Repubblica]], [[La Stampa]] and the weekly [[L'Espresso]]. The television stations ''Radio Tre'', ''[[RAI Due]]'' and ''[[RAI Tre]]'' have hosted many of his discussions on various scientific topics.\n\n== Political Views ==\nOdifreddi was heavily influenced by [[Bertrand Russell]] and [[Noam Chomsky]].<ref>[http://www.piergiorgioodifreddi.it/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/chomsky.pdf Odifreddi interviews Noam Chomsky]</ref> He repeatedly manifested his opposition to US policies, in particular against that of [[George W. Bush]] and [[Israel]], as indicated in his writings ''Non siamo tutti americani'',<ref>[http://web.archive.org/web/20070101155809/http://www.vialattea.net/odifreddi/guerra.htm ''No al terrorismo. Ma quale?'']</ref> ''La dannata Terra Santa''<ref>[http://web.archive.org/web/20140203105738/http://www.teneraerbetta.it/index.php/component/content/article/12-blog-tenera/disappunti-di-viaggio/1707-la-dannata-terra-santa ''La dannata Terra Santa'']</ref> and the controversial ''Intervista a Hitler'',<ref>[http://www.piergiorgioodifreddi.it/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/hitler.pdf Interview with Adolf Hitler]</ref> in his book ''Il matematico impertinente''.\n\nHis views on Israel \"immodestly inspired\" by [[José Saramago]] and Noam Chomsky caused protests, which led to the deletion of an editorial he wrote in his blog at [[la Repubblica]] on November 2012,<ref>[http://www.forumcivico.it/dieci-volte-peggio-dei-nazisti-articolo-censurato-repubblica-885.html ''«Dieci volte peggio dei nazisti» — The censored articled on Repubblica'']</ref> where he talked about [[Operation Pillar of Defense|the Israeli incursion]] in the [[Gaza Strip]].
