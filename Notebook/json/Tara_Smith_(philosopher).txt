Smith''' (born 1961) is a professor of [[philosophy]] and holder of the [[BB&T]] [[Chairman|Chair]] for the Study of [[Objectivism (Ayn Rand)|Objectivism]]<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.utexas.edu/news/2008/03/20/lib_arts_ayn_rand/ |title=BB&T Donates $2 Million for Ayn Rand Research at The University of Texas at Austin &#124; The University of Texas at Austin |publisher=Utexas.edu |date=2008-12-07 |accessdate=2009-04-04}}</ref> and holder of the Anthem Foundation Fellowship for the Study of Objectivism<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.utexas.edu/news/2007/10/01/lib_arts-2/ |title=Anthem Foundation Renews Gift for Ayn Rand Research on 50th Anniversary of “Atlas Shrugged” &#124; The University of Texas at Austin |publisher=Utexas.edu |date= |accessdate=2009-04-04}}</ref> at the [[University of Texas at Austin]].\n\nSmith specializes in moral and political theory. She did her undergraduate work at the [[University of Virginia]] and received her doctorate from [[Johns Hopkins University]]. She is also a contributing author to several essay collections about [[Ayn Rand]]'s novels.
