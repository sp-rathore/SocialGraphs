{"batchcomplete": "", "query": {"normalized": [{"to": "Keith Lehrer", "from": "Keith_Lehrer"}], "pages": {"2254444": {"ns": 0, "pageid": 2254444, "revisions": [{"*": "{{BLP sources|date=September 2010}}\n\n'''Keith Lehrer''' (born January 10, 1936) is Regent's Professor emeritus of [[Philosophy]] at the [[University of Arizona]] and a Research Professor of Philosophy at the [[University of Miami]] in Florida, where he spends half of each academic year.  He joined the faculty at the University of Arizona in 1973, where he helped build a major graduate program.  Prior to that, he taught at the [[University of Rochester]].\n\nLehrer received his [[Ph.D.]] in [[philosophy]] from [[Brown University]] where he studied under [[Richard Taylor (philosopher)|Richard Taylor]] and [[Roderick Chisholm]].  His research interests include [[epistemology]],<ref name=\"Olsson2003\">{{cite book|last=Olsson|first=Erik J.|title=The epistemology of Keith Lehrer|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5qdfk_zVTWwC&pg=PP11|accessdate=7 September 2010|year=2003|publisher=Springer|isbn=978-1-4020-1605-9}}</ref> [[free will]], [[rational consensus]], [[Thomas Reid]] and, recently, [[aesthetics]].\n\nLehrer is a former president of the Pacific Division of the [[American Philosophical Association]] (APA) and also served as the APA executive director for a number of years.  He is an elected Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref>http://uanews.org/story/ua-philosopher-honored</ref>\n\nLehrer's work, \"Why Not Skepticism?\" is used in many introductory philosophy courses as a coherent and readable introduction to the subject. He has authored seven books on philosophical subjects, and over 170 scholarly articles. Lehrer is perhaps best known for his defense of the [[coherentism|coherence]] [[theory of justification]] in epistemology. He is the originator of the widely discussed [http://faculty.fordham.edu/nballantyne/truetemp.html TrueTemp example].\n\nLehrer, and his wife Adrienne Lehrer, are also artists.
