He developed a distinctive philosophical approach which he called \"'''transcendental pragmatics'''\"<!--boldface per WP:R#PLA--> (''Transzendentalpragmatik'').<ref>[[Andreas Dorschel]] (ed.), ''Transzendentalpragmatik'', Suhrkamp, Frankfurt am Main 1993. In 1940 he was a war volunteer with his entire graduating class. After the [[Second World War]] Apel studied from 1945 to 1950 at the [[University of Bonn]], first history and intellectual history, before he committed himself as a student of Erich Rothacker on philosophy.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Hösle|first1=Vittorio|title=Die Krise der Gegenwart und die Verantwortung der Philosophie|date=24 September 1997|publisher=C.H.Beck|isbn=978-3406392740|pages=110–115}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|last1=Reese-Schäfer|first1=Walter|title=Karl-Otto Apel zur Einführung|date=1990|publisher=Junius Verlag|location=Hamburg|isbn=978-3885068617|pages=15–21}}</ref> In 1950, he received his doctorate from Bonn with a thesis on [[Martin Heidegger]].\n\n==Academic positions==\nApel was appointed lecturer at the [[University of Mainz]] in 1961. He was a full professor of philosophy at the [[University of Kiel]] from 1962 to 1969, at the [[University of Saarbrücken]] from 1969 to 1972, and at the [[University of Frankfurt am Main]] from 1972 to 1990.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Deutsche National Bibliothek|title=Apel, Karl-Otto|url=http://d-nb.info/gnd/118503596/about/html|website=http://d-nb.info/|publisher=Deutsche National Biblothek|accessdate=19 August 2014}}</ref> In 1990 he transferred to [[emeritus]] status. He has held a number of visiting and guest professorships at universities around the world.\n\nHe was made a Member of the [[Academia Europaea]] in 1989 and a Full Member of the [[Academia Scientiarum et Artium Europaea]] in 1993. In 2001 he was awarded the [[Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany]].\n\n==Philosophy==\nApel worked in [[ethics]], the [[philosophy of language]] and human sciences. He wrote extensively in these fields, publishing mostly in German. Apel's work brings together the [[analytical philosophy|Analytical]] and [[continental philosophy|Continental]] philosophical traditions, especially [[pragmatism]] and the [[critical theory]] of the [[Frankfurt School]].\n\nIn ''Understanding and Explanation: A Transcendental-Pragmatic Perspective'',<ref>Cambridge, Mass: MIT Press, 1984.</ref> Apel reformulated the difference between understanding (''[[Verstehen]]'') and explanation (''Erklärung''), which originated in the [[hermeneutics]] of [[Wilhelm Dilthey]] and [[interpretive sociology]] of [[Max Weber]], on the basis of a Peircean-inspired transcendental-pragmatic account of language. This account of the \"[[lifeworld]]\" would become an element of the theory of communicative action<ref>Witzany, Guenther (1991). Transzendentalpragmatik und Ek-sistenz. Normenbegruendung - Normendurchsetzung. Verlag Die Blaue Eule, Essen.</ref> and [[discourse ethics]], which Apel co-developed with [[Jürgen Habermas]]. Strategic rationality both claim to stand in need of communicative rationality that is seen as, in several regards, more fundamental.<ref>For an assessment of this claim: Andreas Dorschel, 'Ist strategisches Handeln ergänzungsbedürftig? Karl-Otto Apels These und ihre Begründung', in: ''Archives européenes de sociologie'' XXX (1989), pp. 123-149. [http://journals.cambridge.org/action/displayAbstract?fromPage=online&aid=5461532/]</ref> While sympathetic to Habermas's ''Theory of Communicative Action'', Apel has been critical of aspects of Habermas's approach. Apel has proposed that a theory of communication should be grounded in the transcendental-pragmatic conditions of communication. After taking his point of departure from Apel, Habermas has moved towards a \"weak transcendentalism\" that is more closely tied to empirical social inquiry.\n\nApel has also written works on [[Charles Sanders Peirce]] and is a past president of the C.S. Peirce Society.\n\nAn early German-speaking adversary of so-called critical rationalism, Apel published a critique of the philosophy of [[Karl Raimund Popper]]: In ''Transformation der Philosophie'' (1973), Apel charged Popper with being guilty of, amongst other things, a pragmatic contradiction.<ref>\"Apel, Karl-Otto\", ''La philosophie de A à Z'', by Elizabeth Clement, Chantal Demonque, Laurence Hansen-Love, and Pierre Kahn, Paris, 1994, Hatier, 19-20.</ref><ref>''Towards a Transformation of Philosophy (Marquette Studies in Philosophy, No 20)'', by Karl-Otto Apel, trans., Glyn Adey and David Fisby, Milwaukee, 1998, Marquette University Press.</ref>\n\nFrom the 1970s to the 1990s, Apel influenced some other philosophers writing in Europe, the Americas, and Asia.\n\n==List of works==\n*''Analytic Philosophy of Language and the Geisteswissenschaften'' (1967)\n*''Hermeneutik und Ideologiekritik'' (1971)\n*''Sprache, Brücke und Hindernis'' (1972)\n*''Dialog als Methode'' (1972)\n*''Transformation der philosophie: Sprachanalytik, Semiotik, Hermeneutik'' (1973)\n*''Transformation der philosophie: Das Apriori der Kommunikationsgemeinschaft'' (1976)\n*''Sprachpragmatik und philosophie'' (1976)\n*''Neue Versuche über Erklären und Verstehen'' (1978)\n*''Die Erklären/Verstehen-Kontroverse in Transzendentalpragmatischer Sicht'' (1979)\n*''Towards a Transformation of Philosophy'' (1980 & 1998)\n*''Charles S.
