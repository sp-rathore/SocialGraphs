February 15, 1920 – January 16, 2001) was a French philosopher, Professor of ''[[Epistemology|Philosophy of Knowledge]]'' at the prestigious [[Collège de France]], in [[Paris]], from 1962 to 1990, succeeding to [[Maurice Merleau-Ponty]], and [[Professor emeritus]] from 1991 to 2001.<ref>[http://www.college-de-france.fr/site/professeurs-disparus/jules_vuillemin.htm Collège de France.]</ref> He was an Invited Professor at the [[Institute for Advanced Studies]], Princeton (1968).<ref>[http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/fr/cours-au-college-de-france-et-conferences Institute for Advanced Studies, Princeton (1968).]</ref>\n[[File:College de france pediment.jpg|thumb|[[Collège de France]] ([[Paris]], [[France]]).]]\n[[File:Jules Vuillemin - Photo Philippe Binant.jpg|thumb|Jules Vuillemin, ''La philosophie de l'algèbre'', Presses Universitaires de France, Paris, 1962.]]\n\nAt the Collège de France, Vuillemin introduced [[analytical philosophy]] to [[France]].<ref>See 1962-1990. ''Résumés des cours et travaux'', '''Annuaires du Collège de France''', Paris.</ref> Vuillemin’s thought had a major influence on [[Jacques Bouveresse]]'s works.<ref name=\"Bouveresse’s eulogy\">{{cite web | url=http://www.college-de-france.fr/media/professeurs-disparus/UPL53971_necrovuill.pdf | title=Vuillemin’s eulogy by Jacques Bouveresse | accessdate=May 25, 2012|language=fr}}</ref> Vuillemin himself vindicated the legacy of [[Martial Gueroult]].\n\nA friend of [[Michel Foucault]], he supported his election at the Collège de France, and was also close to [[Michel Serres]].\n\n== Life ==\nAfter studying at the [[Ecole Normale Supérieure]], he completed his [[agrégation]] in 1943, being received [[premier ex aequo]] alongside [[Tran Duc Thao]]. A student of [[Gaston Bachelard]] and [[Jean Cavaillès]], he was however at first influenced by [[Phenomenology (philosophy)|phenomenology]] and [[existentialism]], before shifting towards study of [[logics]] and [[philosophy of science|science]]. In 1962, he published a book titled ''The Philosophy of Algebra'', dedicated to the mathematician [[Pierre Samuel]], a member of the [[Bourbaki]] group, as well as to [[René Thom]], to the physicist [[Raymond Siestrunck]] and to the linguist [[George Vallet]]. Vuillemin thought that any renewal of methods in mathematics influenced philosophy, thus relating the discovery of [[irrational numbers]] to [[platonism]], [[algebraic geometry]] to [[cartesianism]], infinitesimal [[calculus]] to [[Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz|Leibniz]]. Furthermore, he observed that philosophy had not yet taken into account the changes brought to mathematics by [[Joseph Louis Lagrange]] and [[Évariste Galois]].\n\nIn 1968, he co-founded with [[Gilles-Gaston Granger]] the journal ''L’Âge de la Science''.<ref>[http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/sites/poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/files/users/documents/fichier_page/biblio_vuillemin.pdf Bibliography]</ref> He was one of the main commentators of [[Bertrand Russell]], [[Ludwig Wittgenstein]], [[Rudolph Carnap]] and [[Willard Van Orman Quine]] in France.<ref>[http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/fr/archives-jules-vuillemin Jules Vuillemin's Archives, \"Description\".]</ref>\n\nVuillemin also took an interest into [[aesthetics]], beside writing several books on [[Immanuel Kant|Kant]], [[Anselm of Canterbury|Anselm]] or on [[Diodorus's master argument]] (see [[problem of future contingents]]).\n\n== Jules Vuillemin’s Archives ==\nThe [http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/fr/archives-jules-vuillemin Jules Vuillemin's Archives] are located in France at the Laboratoire d'Histoire des Sciences et de Philosophie - Archives [[Henri Poincaré]].<ref>[http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/fr Laboratoire d'Histoire des Sciences et de Philosophie - Archives Henri Poincaré]</ref>\n\n[[Gilles-Gaston Granger]] is the president of the scientific committee of Jules Vuillemin's Archives.<ref>[http://poincare.univ-lorraine.fr/fr/comite-scientifique-des-archives-jules-vuillemin Jules Vuillemin's Archives.]</ref>\n\n== Bibliography ==\n* ''Le Sens du destin'', en collaboration avec [[Louis Guillermit]], Neuchâtel, Éditions de La Baconnière, 1948.\n* ''Essai sur la signification de la mort'', Paris, PUF, 1948.\n* ''L'Être et le travail.
