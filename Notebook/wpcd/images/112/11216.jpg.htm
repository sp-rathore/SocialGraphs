Larsen_B_Collapse.jpg‎ (540 × 498 pixel, file size: 87 KB, MIME type: image/jpeg)

Shows the 2002 breakup of the Larsen B Ice Shelf in comparison of the US state of Rhode Island. This region covering appromiately 3250 km2 with 200 m thick ice had been continuously glaciated since before the end of the last glacial period.

Copyright

This image is based off of NASA Terra/MODIS imagery which belongs to the public domain. The composition of this with an outline of Rhode Island is an original work produced by Robert A. Rohde and is incorporated into the Global Warming Art project.
