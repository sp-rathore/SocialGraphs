Summary

Description: Galaxies, galaxies everywhere - as far as NASA's Hubble Space Telescope can see. This view of nearly 12,000 galaxies is the deepest visible-light image of the cosmos. Called the Hubble Ultra Deep Field, this galaxy-studded view represents a "deep" core sample of the universe, cutting across billions of light-years.

Credit: NASA, ESA, S. Beckwith (STScI) and the HUDF Team

Source: http://hubblesite.org/newscenter/newsdesk/archive/releases/2004/07/image/a
