Description Ilha de Mozambique was first occupied by Portuguese explorers in the late 1400s. They quickly estblished a fort there, and with time quite a community sprang up, including a church, mosque, shops, and residences. This church is one of the architectural remains there. The island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is off the eastern coast of Africa and is part of the country of Mozambique.
