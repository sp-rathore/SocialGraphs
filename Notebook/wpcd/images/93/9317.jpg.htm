Copycenter

The way it was characterized politically, you had copyright, which is what the big companies use to lock everything up; you had copyleft, which is free software's way of making sure they can't lock it up; and then Berkeley had what we called ‘copycenter’, which is ‘take it down to the copy centre and make as many copies as you want’. — Kirk McKusick, BSDCon 1999.
