Demographics of Guinea-Bissau

2007 Schools Wikipedia Selection. Related subjects: African Geography

The population of Guinea-Bissau is ethnically diverse with distinct languages, customs, and social structures. Most Guineans, 99%, are blacks — mostly Fula and Mandinka-speaker concentrated in the north and northeast, the Balanta and Papel, living in the southern coastal regions, and the Manjaco and Mancanha, occupying the central and northern coastal areas. Most of the rest are mestiços of mixed Portuguese and black descent, including Cape Verdean minority. Pure Portuguese are less than 1% of the Guinea-Bissauans, which caused by the exodus of most Portuguese settlers after independence. Most people are farmers, with traditional religious beliefs ( animism); 45% are Muslim, principally Fula and Mandinka. Lower than 8% are Christians, mostly Roman Catholics.
